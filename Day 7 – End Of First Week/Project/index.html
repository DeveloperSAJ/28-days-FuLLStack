How human brain works

Exploring the science of thought and intelligence

From processing sensory information to forming lifelong memories, the brain coordinates every action and emotion. Discover how this complex network of neurons powers learning, reasoning, creativity, and consciousness.

Main Brain Parts

1: Cerebrum

The largest part of the brain, responsible for voluntary activities, sensory perception, and cognitive functions.

It controls:

  • Thinking
  • Memory
  • Emotions
  • Speech
  • Movement
  • Decision Making

It is responsible for intelligence and conscious actions. The outer layer of the cerebrum is called the cerebral cortex.

2: Cerebellum

The cerebellum is located at the back of the brain, under the cerebrum.It is responsible for motor control, coordination, and balance.

It controls:

  • Balance
  • Coordination
  • Posture
  • Fine motor skills (like writing or typing)

It helps make movements smooth and precise.

3: Brainstem

The brainstem connects the brain to the spinal cord. It is responsible for basic life functions like breathing, heart rate, and sleep.

It controls:

  • Breathing
  • Heart rate
  • Sleep
  • Wakefulness
  • Balance
  • Coordination

It keeps you alive by managing vital body functions without you thinking about them.

Memory Systems

1: Sensory Memory

Sensory memory is the brain’s very short-term storage that lasts only a few seconds.

  • It holds information from your senses (sight, sound, touch, smell, taste) just long enough for the brain to decide if it’s important.
  • Example: Seeing a flash of light and remembering it for a brief moment.

2: Short-Term Memory

Short-term memory temporarily stores information you are currently thinking about or using.

  • Usually lasts about 20–30 seconds without repetition.
  • Can hold 5–9 items at once (like a phone number you just heard).
  • Example: Remembering a new name right after someone tells you.

3: Long-Term Memory

Long-term memory is where information is stored for hours, days, or even a lifetime.

  • Includes facts, experiences, skills, and knowledge.
  • Can be explicit (conscious) or implicit (unconscious skills).
  • Example: Remembering how to ride a bike or your first day at school.

Interesting Facts

  • The human brain weighs about 1.4 kilograms (3 pounds).
  • It contains around 86 billion neurons.
  • The brain consumes roughly 20% of the body’s energy even though it’s only 2% of body weight.
  • Brain cells, called neurons, can send signals up to 120 meters per second.
  • The brain is more active at night than during the day.
  • Memory storage is not in one spot; different areas store different types of memories.
  • Your brain generates about 50,000 thoughts per day.
  • The human brain has more connections than there are stars in the Milky Way.
  • It can process an image in as little as 13 milliseconds.
  • Laughing, exercise, and learning all increase brain activity and create new neural connections.
  • The brain can reorganize itself after injury — this is called neuroplasticity.
  • Sleep is essential: lack of sleep can negatively affect memory, learning, and mood.
  • The brain contains 75% water, which helps it function properly.
  • Emotions are processed in the amygdala, while logical thinking happens in the prefrontal cortex.
  • The brain can store limitless amounts of information, unlike a computer.
